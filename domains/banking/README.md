# Banking Domain – Business Analyst Guide

## 1. What a Business Analyst typically works on in this domain
- Customer onboarding, account opening, and KYC process optimization.
- Product performance for deposits, loans, credit cards, and payments.
- Risk and compliance reporting with business stakeholders.
- Digital adoption and branch/channel efficiency.

## 2. Common business problems
- High drop‑off during onboarding or loan applications.
- Low cross‑sell/upsell rates for credit products.
- Rising delinquency or early‑stage defaults.
- Branch cost inefficiencies vs. digital channels.

## 3. Common KPIs and metrics
- Account opening conversion rate, application completion rate.
- Loan approval rate, delinquency rate, NPL ratio.
- Net interest margin, fee income per customer.
- Digital active users, branch transactions per FTE.

## 4. Typical data sources
- Core banking systems and loan servicing platforms.
- CRM and customer onboarding platforms.
- Risk, fraud, and compliance reporting systems.
- Channel analytics for mobile, web, ATM, and branch.

## 5. How this domain uses the standard BA workflow
- **Problem framing:** define product or process issue and target outcomes.
- **Data collection:** extract application, transaction, and risk datasets.
- **Cleaning:** standardize customer IDs and product codes; handle duplicates.
- **Analysis:** funnel analysis, cohort performance, credit risk segmentation.
- **Insights:** identify drop‑off drivers and ROI of process changes.
- **Communication:** present recommendations to business, risk, and ops leaders.
