# Telecom Domain – Business Analyst Guide

## 1. What a Business Analyst typically works on in this domain
- Subscriber growth, retention, and churn reduction initiatives.
- Pricing and plan optimization across prepaid/postpaid offerings.
- Network performance impacts on customer experience and revenue.
- Product and bundle performance (voice, data, roaming, OTT add‑ons).

## 2. Common business problems
- Rising churn in specific regions or customer segments.
- Low adoption of premium data plans or value‑added services.
- ARPU decline due to competitive pricing or heavy discounting.
- Customer complaints tied to network outages or dropped calls.

## 3. Common KPIs and metrics
- Churn rate, retention rate, net adds.
- ARPU (Average Revenue Per User), MRR.
- Usage metrics: data consumption, call minutes, SMS.
- NPS/CSAT, complaint rate, outage impact.

## 4. Typical data sources
- CRM and subscriber management systems.
- Billing and usage (CDR) data warehouses.
- Network performance logs and outage reports.
- Customer support/ticketing systems.

## 5. How this domain uses the standard BA workflow
- **Problem framing:** define churn or ARPU issues, impacted segments, and targets.
- **Data collection:** pull subscriber cohorts, usage, billing, and network KPIs.
- **Cleaning:** reconcile customer IDs, handle missing usage data, standardize plans.
- **Analysis:** cohort churn analysis, plan performance, network quality impact.
- **Insights:** quantify drivers (price sensitivity, coverage gaps) and revenue impact.
- **Communication:** deliver action plan for pricing, retention offers, or network fixes.
