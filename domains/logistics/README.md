# Logistics Domain – Business Analyst Guide

## 1. What a Business Analyst typically works on in this domain
- Transportation performance and route optimization.
- Warehouse throughput and order fulfillment efficiency.
- Cost‑to‑serve and carrier performance management.
- SLA compliance and delivery reliability.

## 2. Common business problems
- Late deliveries and missed SLAs.
- High transportation costs or inefficient routes.
- Warehouse congestion or picking errors.
- Capacity planning issues during peak demand.

## 3. Common KPIs and metrics
- On‑time delivery rate, SLA adherence.
- Cost per shipment, cost per mile.
- Order cycle time, pick/pack accuracy.
- Utilization of fleet or warehouse capacity.

## 4. Typical data sources
- TMS and WMS systems.
- Carrier invoices and tracking data.
- Fleet telematics and GPS data.
- Customer service delivery issue logs.

## 5. How this domain uses the standard BA workflow
- **Problem framing:** define service or cost objective and scope.
- **Data collection:** gather shipment, route, and warehouse data.
- **Cleaning:** align shipment IDs, normalize carrier codes.
- **Analysis:** route efficiency, SLA root causes, warehouse bottlenecks.
- **Insights:** quantify savings and service improvements.
- **Communication:** share recommendations with ops and carrier teams.
