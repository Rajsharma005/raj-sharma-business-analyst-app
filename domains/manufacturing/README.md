# Manufacturing Domain – Business Analyst Guide

## 1. What a Business Analyst typically works on in this domain
- Production efficiency, throughput, and capacity utilization.
- Supply chain performance and inventory optimization.
- Quality control and defect reduction initiatives.
- Cost analysis across plants, lines, or SKUs.

## 2. Common business problems
- High scrap or defect rates.
- Bottlenecks causing missed production targets.
- Inventory imbalances and stockouts of critical materials.
- Variability in vendor performance or lead times.

## 3. Common KPIs and metrics
- OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness).
- Yield, scrap rate, defect rate.
- Cycle time, throughput, on‑time delivery.
- Inventory turns, supplier lead time adherence.

## 4. Typical data sources
- MES/SCADA systems and production logs.
- ERP systems for inventory and procurement.
- Quality management systems (QMS).
- Maintenance and downtime logs.

## 5. How this domain uses the standard BA workflow
- **Problem framing:** define production or quality objective and scope.
- **Data collection:** pull production, quality, and supply chain data.
- **Cleaning:** align SKU/line IDs, handle sensor gaps, standardize timestamps.
- **Analysis:** bottleneck analysis, yield trends, supplier performance.
- **Insights:** quantify cost of quality issues and throughput gains.
- **Communication:** recommend process changes to ops and plant leadership.
